# Live Edge Table

When we moved to our new apartment, I wanted to get a live edge table but most of the ones I could find were (understandably) quite expensive. One day, we went to a thrift store called [The Big Reuse](https://bigreuse.org/pages/big-reuse-center-shop) at the recommendation of [Climate Town](https://www.youtube.com/c/ClimateTown/videos) to look for furniture and kitchen items. We discovered that not only did they have a section of the store dedicated to live edge wood slabs, but they had cuts of beautiful [spalted](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spalting) sycamore wood that were the perfect size for our apartment! 

We picked out the perfect piece and carried it home on the subway. Now I just needed to turn this slab of wood into a table.

<img src="/carrying-wood-slab.webp" alt="James carrying the slab of wood outside the thrift store" width="80%"/>

I bought some hairpin table legs online and an electric sander and drill off facebook marketplace. I pre-drilled holes to screw the legs onto the base, then sanded, and sanded, and sanded the top of the table until it was perfectly smooth. 

I cleaned all the sawdust out of our apartment bathroom and finally, it was time to apply the finish (luckily I still had plenty of finish left over from my lathe projects). I'm pretty happy with the final result:
